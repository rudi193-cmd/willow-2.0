# briefing_draft — failure modes the review surface must catch

verify_class=containment: the draft renders to the operator, it never acts.
The damage mode is misinforming a human, ranked:

1. **Invented or mutated numbers.** The one unforgivable failure — a
   briefing that says "12 promoted" when the report says 4 trains the
   operator to distrust every future receipt. `grounded`-style spot checks
   in fixtures pin the copied-numbers rule; the renderer should also print
   the raw report on expand, so lies are one click from exposure.
2. **Burying the anomaly.** pass_errors present but the headline celebrates
   compost counts. Fixtures pin anomaly-first ordering and the tone rule.
   This is the shape-only-status-light failure in prose form.
3. **Invented urgency.** attention_items containing healthy numbers or
   generic advice ("consider monitoring Postgres"). The prompt restricts
   attention items to operator-actionable facts; reviewer taste enforces
   the rest.
4. **Fleet dialect leak.** "W19CD", "norn", "draugr" without translation.
   Cosmetic here, fatal in the eventual any-laptop product — this harness
   doubles as the rehearsal space for domestic language.
5. **Tone miscalibration.** red requires a missing primary effect, not a
   scary-looking error string. The tone rule in the prompt is mechanical;
   fixtures pin one case per tone.
