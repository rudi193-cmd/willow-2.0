SECTION 1: CONFIRMED LATE DEATHS — SCIENTISTS WHO LIVED INTO 2000s-2010s
================================================================================

The following Generation-1 Paperclip scientists had documented deaths after 2000,
living well beyond average life expectancy:

--- DIETER GRAU (April 24, 1913 – December 17, 2014) ---
CONFIRMED DEAD. Age at death: 101.
Role: Quality assurance director for Saturn I and Saturn V rockets; core member of
von Braun's rocket group. Arrived USA via Operation Paperclip from Peenemunde.
Death location: Huntsville, Alabama.
Status verified by: Legacy.com obituary, Wikipedia, AL.com (Huntsville).
Sources: Dieter Grau Obituary (Legacy.com), Wikipedia/Dieter_Grau.

--- ERNST STUHLINGER (December 19, 1913 – May 25, 2008) ---
CONFIRMED DEAD. Age at death: 94.
Role: Guidance systems developer, later director of MSFC Space Science Laboratory
(1960-1968), MSFC associate director for science (1968-1975). Pioneer of ion engine
research for long-endurance spaceflight. PhD student under Werner Heisenberg.
Death location: Huntsville, Alabama (in failing health for several months prior).
Status verified by: Find a Grave memorial, SpaceRef obituary, Wikipedia.
NOTE: Stuhlinger gave multiple recorded oral history interviews (see Section 6).
Sources: Ernst Stuhlinger Find a Grave, SpaceRef obituary notice.

--- KONRAD DANNENBERG (February 4, 1912 – February 16, 2009) ---
CONFIRMED DEAD. Age at death: 96.
Role: Rocket propulsion pioneer, Saturn V development. Received NASA Exceptional
Service Medal 1973. Held by British briefly as potential war criminal before being
recruited for Paperclip.
Death location: Huntsville, Alabama.
Status verified by: Legacy.com obituary, Physics Today AIP obituary, Wikipedia.
NOTE: Dannenberg co-conducted oral history interviews of other Paperclip scientists.
Sources: Konrad Dannenberg (Legacy.com), Physics Today AIP obituary.

--- WALTER HAEUSSERMANN (March 2, 1914 – December 8, 2010) ---
CONFIRMED DEAD. Age at death: 96.
Role: Director of Guidance and Control Laboratory at MSFC; developed guidance and
control systems for Redstone, Jupiter, and Saturn program (Astrionics Laboratory).
Death cause: Complications from a fall.
Death location: Huntsville, Alabama.
Status verified by: Legacy.com obituary (AL.com, December 14, 2010), Wikipedia.
Sources: Walter Haeussermann Obituary (Legacy.com), Philosophy of Science Portal notice.

--- HANS FICHTNER (September 8, 1917 – October 21, 2012) ---
CONFIRMED DEAD. Age at death: 95.
Role: Rocket scientist, worked on V-2 rockets under von Braun at Peenemunde from
1939-1945, later brought to USA via Paperclip.
Death location: Huntsville, Alabama area.
Status verified by: Wikipedia, AstroNautix entry.

--- RUDOLF "RUDI" SCHLIDT (1914 – September 28, 2012) ---
CONFIRMED DEAD. Age at death: approximately 97-98.
Role: Member of von Braun's Paperclip rocket team.
Death location: Huntsville, Alabama.

--- OSCAR HOLDERER (1919 – May 5, 2015) ---
CONFIRMED DEAD. Age at death: 95.
Role: Low-level engineer under von Braun; designed the wind tunnel used for Saturn V
testing at MSFC; after retirement designed exhibits including multi-axis trainer and
one-sixth gravity chair for U.S. Space & Rocket Center.
Death cause: Stroke a few days prior.
Death location: Huntsville, Alabama.
Survivors: Second wife Jan Smith Dunlap Holderer; stepchildren Clifford Dunlap and
Mary Gaither.
STATUS NOTE: In 2015, multiple outlets (Boston Globe, Mercury News, San Diego Union-
Tribune) reported Holderer as "the last known member" of the original German moon
rocket design team. This claim was applied specifically to the core von Braun Paperclip
group that arrived in 1945. See discrepancy note below under Section 5.
Sources: Oscar Holderer Wikipedia, Legacy.com obituary, Boston Globe obituary.

--- GEORG VON TIESENHAUSEN (May 18, 1914 – June 3, 2018) ---
CONFIRMED DEAD. Age at death: 104.
Role: Rocket scientist; designed test stands for V-2 engines at Peenemunde; arrived
USA 1953 (Operation Paperclip second wave); worked on Redstone at Redstone Arsenal;
originated concept for the Lunar Rover (1959, initially rejected); worked through
Apollo and into Shuttle era.
Death location: His home in Huntsville, Alabama.
Additional note: Neil Armstrong made a rare public appearance in 2011 to present von
Tiesenhausen with a lifetime achievement award at the U.S. Space & Rocket Center.
Holocaust Memorial Museum (USHMM) holds an oral history interview with him.
STATUS NOTE: Von Tiesenhausen was officially designated the LAST LIVING NASA scientist
to have worked on Nazi rockets and been brought to the US under Operation Paperclip.
Sources: Vice article, TechTimes article, Georg von Tiesenhausen Wikipedia.

--- FRIEDWARDT WINTERBERG (born June 12, 1929) ---
STATUS: LIKELY STILL ALIVE as of 2026. Age: approximately 96.
Role: German-American theoretical physicist. PhD from Max Planck Institute, Gottingen
(1955), under Werner Heisenberg. Brought to USA in 1959 via Operation Paperclip.
Hired at University of Nevada, Reno as associate professor of physics (1963),
promoted to full professor (1968); holds title of research professor emeritus.
Research fields: General relativity, Planck scale physics, nuclear fusion,
inertial confinement fusion, plasmas. Over 260 publications and three books.
EVIDENCE OF CONTINUED ACTIVITY:
- Wikipedia article last updated September 22, 2025; uses present tense ("is"),
  no death date recorded.
- Published in Physics Essays journal in 2023-2024 ("The Einstein myth and the
  crisis in modern physics").
- Google Scholar profile active with 2,348 citations.
- viXra.org lists active preprint submissions.
- PeoplePill.com bio notation: "German physicist (1929–)" with no end date.
CAVEAT: No source positively confirms he was alive in early 2026; the absence of
a death notice combined with recent publications in 2023-2024 makes him a strong
candidate for still being alive. No obituary, death notice, or university memorial
found anywhere.
CLARIFICATION ON PAPERCLIP MEMBERSHIP: Winterberg's association with Paperclip is
specifically his 1959 arrival. He was not in the original 1945-1946 cohort; he is
a later-wave recruit. He worked independently and is not a member of the "von Braun
rocket group" per se, but is formally a Paperclip scientist.
Sources: Friedwardt Winterberg Wikipedia, University of Nevada Reno profile (2009),
PeoplePill biography, Physics Essays publication 2023-2024.

CONCLUSION ON GENERATION-1 SURVIVORSHIP: Among the well-documented rocket/aerospace
Paperclip scientists, Georg von Tiesenhausen (d. 2018) appears to be the last confirmed
death of a core member. Friedwardt Winterberg (b. 1929, physicist) appears to be the
only plausible surviving Generation-1 Paperclip scientist as of 2026, if still alive.

SECTION 3: THE "LAST SURVIVOR" QUESTION — CLARIFYING THE DISCREPANCY
================================================================================

Two scientists were each identified by the media as "the last survivor," creating
apparent contradiction:

OSCAR HOLDERER (d. May 2015, age 95):
- The Boston Globe, Mercury News, and multiple outlets in May 2015 called him
  "the last known member of the German moon rocket design team."
- This likely referred specifically to the original 1945-1946 wave of Paperclip
  recruits who arrived at Fort Bliss with von Braun.

GEORG VON TIESENHAUSEN (d. June 2018, age 104):
- Vice, TechTimes, WAFF, and Scotsman in June 2018 called him "the last living
  NASA scientist to have worked on Nazi rockets after being brought to the US
  under Operation Paperclip."
- Von Tiesenhausen arrived in the US in 1953, a later Paperclip wave than
  Holderer's 1945 arrival.

RESOLUTION: The discrepancy is definitional. Holderer was the last of the
1945-cohort (those who arrived immediately after the war). Von Tiesenhausen,
arriving eight years later in 1953, outlived Holderer by three years and died
in 2018 as the last of any Paperclip Peenemunde veteran in the US program.
Von Tiesenhausen is the confirmed overall last survivor of the Paperclip
rocket scientist cohort (Peenemunde connection) in any wave.

FRIEDWARDT WINTERBERG remains the only possibly still-living Paperclip scientist
as of 2026, as a physicist (not rocket engineer) recruited in 1959.

SECTION 4: GENERATION-2 DESCENDANTS — CHILDREN OF PAPERCLIP SCIENTISTS

================================================================================

This generation is largely still alive. The von Braun family is the most
documented case.

--- WERNHER VON BRAUN'S CHILDREN ---

IRIS CAREEN VON BRAUN (December 9, 1948 – April 17, 2013)
STATUS: DECEASED. Died of cancer at age 60 in Huntsville, Alabama.
She was the eldest of von Braun's three children, born at Fort Bliss Army Hospital
(El Paso, TX). Worked as a business executive in Huntsville; active philanthropist;
supporter of United Way and Huntsville Hospital Foundation.
NOTE: She predecased her own mother (Maria von Braun, who died 2025).
Sources: StartnewsWire "Iris Careen Von Braun" profile, various family references.

MARGRIT CECILE VON BRAUN (born May 8, 1952, Huntsville, Alabama)
STATUS: ALIVE AND ACTIVE as of early 2026. Age: approximately 73.
Occupation: Professor emerita of environmental engineering, University of Idaho.
Co-founder and director of Terragraphics International Foundation (TIFO), a 501(c)(3)
non-profit focused on environmental justice and hazard management. Current focus:
environmental justice and education at the Duck Valley Indian Reservation
(Nevada/Idaho border). Married to Ian von Lindern.
PUBLIC ACTIVITIES (2024-2025):
- Guest speaker, Space Explorers Celebration, U.S. Space & Rocket Center,
  October 2024. Talk titled "Reaching for the Moon: A Family Adventure."
- Recipient of inaugural Founders Award from U.S. Space & Rocket Center.
- Referenced in Huntsville Business Journal article March 25, 2025 about von
  Braun's economic legacy.
- Quoted on Artemis program: "He would be proud that we're going back to the moon
  with the Artemis program."
Sources: Margrit von Braun Wikipedia, 256today.com Space Explorers Celebration
announcement, Huntsville Business Journal 2025.

PETER CONSTANTINE VON BRAUN (born 1960)
STATUS: BELIEVED ALIVE. Age: approximately 65.
He is Wernher von Braun's only son. Limited public profile.
Known prior occupations: principal and board of education member in Greenwich, CT;
real estate work. No current high-profile role documented.
Notable: Was present at the inaugural Founders Award ceremony at U.S. Space &
Rocket Center. Appeared in 1969 MSFC employee picnic photograph at UAH Archives.
Sources: Wernher von Braun Wikipedia, New Award to Von Braun Family (HvillBlast).

--- MARIA VON BRAUN (WIFE) ---
Maria Luise von Quistorp von Braun (born 1928 – January 20, 2025)
STATUS: RECENTLY DECEASED. Age at death: 96. Died at her home in Alexandria, VA,
surrounded by family. She was Wernher's wife (married March 1, 1947, under military
guard in Landshut). After his death in 1977 she remained in Alexandria; became a yoga
instructor; known as "second grandmother" to neighborhood children. NSS Society
Governor. Survived by her three children (Iris had predeceased her).
NOTE: Her death in January 2025 was described by local media as marking the end of
an era for the German rocket scientists' generation.
Sources: Huntsville Business Journal Feb 10 2025, 256 Today Feb 2025, NSS notice,
Find a Grave memorial #279488972.
